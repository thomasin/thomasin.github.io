How did the 'process over product' concept affect the way you tackled the site redesign and rebuild?

This is something I've always struggled with, I have very high standards while simultaneously struggling to achieve them. This can turn my coding into a long, exhuasting process. I do believe that the things I'm forced to learn while trying to make everything as perfect and simple as I can are very useful. However, this time around I wanted to focus on learning and in particular media queries which I've never used before. To do this, instead of designing a bloated website that would take a lot of my time for me to be satisfied with I designed one that was highly simple, I loved almost straight away, and left a lot of time for me to perfect media queries and understand how they worked.

What did you think about meditation before reading chapter 2?

I've always thought of meditation as partner-in-crime to working out, one trains your body (mostly) and the other trains your mind. I thought of it as a beautiful practice, and I knew if I put effort and time into it the rewards I would get in terms of stress relief and better moods would be incredible but I could never get the motivation.

What new things have you learnt about meditation?

That even the tiniest amount a day will make a difference. I loved the part about scientific inquiry being important, and meditation being so supported by studies and relevant to everyone no matter who they are!

Did any of the suggested meditation techniques stand out to you?

Creating an intention for your meditation and mindfullness. I like the thought that thinking an intention every day can influence yourself to achieving it. I also really want to practice experiencing, not judging or reacting. That is something I struggle with and it's really important to me.

Any other musings?

I want to have calm, clarity and peace of mind every single day! That sounds like it would be wonderful.
