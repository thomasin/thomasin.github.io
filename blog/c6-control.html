When programming, what are some of your triggers? What causes a disproportionate emotional response?

I don't have very many negative emotions when I'm programming, I just zone out from myself and it's like I'm in a bubble where I have endless patience it can take me forever to solve a problem and I'll just keep going happily or put it down and return later. Sometimes I do get minor frustration when I want to do something and there's going to be no simple solution, or the tiniest error is giving me grief and I can't figure out why but they're very mild reactions luckily, I think my mind sees it as an escape from stress.

How did you find the Siberian North Railroad exercise (Stop, Breathe, Notice, Reflect, Respond)?

It was pretty intense! I was really glad I'd done it though, I am a very reactive person and I'll usually just follow my emotions without question which has led to some pretty unnecessary conflict in my life. It was really interesting to feel my emotions well up and then go slowly back down again. I especially liked the Notice part of the reflection, where you can feel all of your feelings in your body. Definitely going to try use this as often as I get the chance!
